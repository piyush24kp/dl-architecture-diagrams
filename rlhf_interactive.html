03
Preference Data
Humans compare two responses
Same prompt → two model outputs
Labeller picks the better one
Dataset: (prompt, y_chosen, y_rejected)
✅
Comparison is

04
Reward Model
Train a scalar "goodness" predictor
Takes (prompt, response) → score
Trained on preference pairs
Loss: -log σ(r(yw) - r(yl))
✅
RM replaces
human judges
at RL scale
(millions of calls)
05
PPO Setup
4 models used simultaneously
Policy (trained), Reference (frozen)
Reward model, Value model
KL penalty keeps policy near reference
✅
KL divergence
prevents reward
hacking —
critical!
06
PPO Training
Policy gradient optimisation
Generate → score → update policy
KL penalty: don't drift too far
R = r_θ(x,y) − β·KL[π_θ ‖ π_ref]
✅
β controls the
KL tradeoff
Too high β:
no improvement
07
DPO: Simpler Path

No reward model, no PPO needed
Fine-tune directly on preferences
-log σ(β·log π(yw)/π_ref(yw) − β·log π(yl)/π_ref(yl))

~= RLHF quality
much simpler
to implement
