ATTENTION
WHY IT WINS · WHERE IT HURTS
summary

✓ Advantages

  • +Long-range context. Any token can attend to any other in one hop — no information decay over distance.
  • +Fully parallel. No recurrence, so the whole sequence trains at once on GPUs — far faster than RNNs.
  • +Interpretable. Attention weights show what looks at what — a window into the model.
  • +Content-based routing. Relevance is learned dynamically per input, not fixed by position.

⚠ Challenges

  • !O(n²) cost. Compute & memory grow quadratically with sequence length — long contexts get expensive.
  • !No order built in. Needs positional encodings; permutation-invariant on its own.
  • !Memory heavy. KV cache at inference grows with context — drives Flash / sparse / linear-attention research.
  • !Data hungry. Few inductive biases means it needs large datasets to generalise well.
