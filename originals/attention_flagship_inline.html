Takeaways
why attention wins · where it hurts · how it's used

✓ Advantages

  • +Long-range context. Any token attends to any other in a single hop — no information decay over distance.
  • +Fully parallel. The whole sequence is processed at once — trains far faster than recurrent models on GPUs.
  • +Interpretable. Attention weights reveal what looks at what — a rare window into the model.
  • +Content-based routing. Relevance is learned dynamically per input, not fixed by position.

⚠ Challenges

  • !O(n²) cost. Compute and memory grow quadratically with sequence length — long contexts get expensive.
  • !No order built in. Self-attention is permutation-invariant; it needs positional encodings.
  • !Memory-heavy. The KV cache at inference grows with context length.
  • !Data-hungry. Few inductive biases means it needs large datasets to generalise well.

◆ Where it's used

  • ›LLMs. GPT, BERT, T5, LLaMA, Claude, Gemini — the entire Transformer family.
  • ›Vision. ViT, CLIP, DINO, SAM — patches as tokens with global attention.
  • ›Generative. Cross-attention conditions Stable Diffusion / DALL·E on text.
  • ›Speech & multimodal. Whisper, AudioLM, Flamingo, GPT-4o.

⚡ Variants & improvements

  • ›FlashAttention. IO-aware kernel — exact result, O(n) memory, much faster.
  • ›MQA / GQA. Share K/V across heads → smaller cache, faster decoding (LLaMA-2/3).
  • ›Sparse / sliding-window. Longformer, BigBird, Mistral — attend locally to cut O(n²).
  • ›Linear attention & RoPE/ALiBi. Performer for O(n); rotary/ALiBi for length generalisation.

✗ Common pitfalls

  • ✗Missing causal mask in a decoder — the model peeks at future tokens and "cheats" during training.
  • ✗Dropping the √dₖ scaling — large dot products saturate softmax and gradients vanish.
  • ✗Wrong padding mask — attending to PAD tokens silently corrupts the output.
  • ✗Train/inference length mismatch in positional encodings — extrapolation degrades quality.
  • ✗Head reshape bugs — mixing up (batch, heads, seq, d_k) dims fails silently, not loudly.
