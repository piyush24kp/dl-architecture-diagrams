The Core Idea

02
Q, K, V Projections
Three linear projections
Q = "what am I looking for?"
K = "what do I contain?"
V = "what do I offer?"
Q=xW_Q K=xW_K V=xW_V
✅
W_Q, W_K, W_V
are learned
during training
d_k = d/h
03
Dot-Product Scores
Q · Kᵀ = similarity scores
Each Q dot-products with all Ks
High score = high relevance
score(Q,K) = Q · Kᵀ
✅
Cosine similarity
in high-dim
space — angle
= relatedness
04
Scale + Softmax
Divide by √d_k then softmax
Without scale: large d_k → tiny gradients
Softmax converts scores → probabilities
softmax(Q·Kᵀ / √d_k)
✅
√d_k prevents
softmax from
saturating in
high dimensions
05
Attention Weights
Sentence: "The bank by the river"
Where each word looks
"bank" strongly attends to "river"
→ it's a river bank, not a financial bank
Rows sum to 1.0 (softmax)
✅
This is why
attention solves
word sense
disambiguation
06
Weighted Sum of V
Multiply weights × V vectors

"bank" output is a mix of "bank"+"river"
